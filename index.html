01 · Motivation

Why This
Project?

Formula 1 betting markets process enormous amounts of information before each race, yet they remain partially inefficient. Bookmakers set odds to guarantee profit through overround margins — not to reflect true probabilities.

This project tests a core hypothesis: qualifying telemetry contains predictive signals the market systematically underprices. Measurable gaps between model-derived and market-implied probabilities represent exploitable Expected Value (EV) opportunities.

06 · Limitations & Future Work

What Could
Be Better?

Known Limitations

  • Podium label is a proxy (Grid ≤ 3 AND market prob > 35%), not actual race results. P4–20 podium finishers are missed.
  • Partial circularity: Grid Position in both label and features. AUC drops to ~0.87 without it.
  • EV threshold of 0.05 is a design choice, not statistically derived.
  • Odds scraper depends on page structure — any layout change requires manual fallback.

Future Extensions

  • Replace proxy labels with actual race results via Ergast or OpenF1 API.
  • Add tyre strategy, weather, and FP pace as additional features.
  • Derive EV threshold empirically via rolling walk-forward backtest.
  • Extend to head-to-head markets and constructor championship odds.
  • Add Platt scaling calibration layer on top of the ensemble.
